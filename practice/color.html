Analogous


Analogous color palettes set themselves apart from other options because-- unlike complementary and triadic-- the colors are all very close to eachother on the color wheel. An analogous color palette is where you take the colors surrounding the base color, which forms a sort of gradient.


Complementary


Complementary colors are colors that are directly opposite from eachother on the color wheel. They create very good contrast from eachother and can help draw the eye to certain key parts of your site.


Triadic


Triadic color palettes are when you take three colors from the color wheel that are all equally far away from one another, which should form a triangle.
