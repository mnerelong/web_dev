Movement

Backflips, slides, whiplash, and more.

Backflip

The most important unexplained mechanic in Rain World is the backflip. Upon preforming a backflip, the player is put in a state where they are able to throw spears vertically (up or down). To carry out a successful backflip there is a mechanic in the games code that sets the time frame in which the backflip must be done. After the player simply walks forward for a not so extended period of time (aproximately three tiles but most likely less) if they turn abruptly they will recieve a smallish speed boost that lasts just a small duration. If the player jumps right after the boost they will have successfully done a backflip! These can be useful in many ways, including traversing difficult rooms, accessing difficult rooms, and can turn a fight the outcome of a fight in your favor.


The speed boost mentioned beforehand can also be used for basic sprinting. By taking advantage of this and then hopping continuously afterwards you can maintain the extra speed for an increased amount of time.



Slide

Another of the most simple mechanics in the game is the slide. The basic slide allows the player to get across large spaces quickly (*if done right ) and is also useful in the entering of tunnels or pipes. To slide, the player must crouch and jump nearly simultaneously and hold forward for the correct length (typically holding forward until the slide is over is acceptable, but fancier slides require different inputs). If the direction is not held long enough then you will have wasted your time.



Roll

One more important basic is rolling. When slugcat falls from up high it will usually be either incapacitated temporarily or dead. This can be avoided by holding down and a direction. There is a minimum of about four tiles (vertically) to do a roll, otherwise you will probably just end up laying on the ground. Max roll length is approximately three rolls or-- more accurately-- four thud sounds. After this, if the player does just jump, the roll will end. If they player does jump, they will leap (similar to a crouch leap) and when they land they will do another ~three rolls.

Rolls can also be entered from backflips and slide variants and are very useful for exploration.

Ways slugcat can enter a roll:

  • Backflips
  • Slide-pounces
  • Slide-flips
  • I'm sure there's more but I can't think of any :(

Whiplash

Whiplash is not a single move*, but a state some techniques put the slugcat in. It is what allows the player to throw vertically (up or down) like seen in backflips. Whiplash can be entered in several different ways. Whiplash is only possible while the slugcat is in the air (or at least mostly in the air), otherwise it will be canceled.
