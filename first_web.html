Movement

Backflips, slides, whiplash, and more.

The most important unexplained mechanic in Rain World is the backflip. Upon preforming a backflip, the player is put in a state where they are able to throw spears vertically (up or down). To carry out a successful backflip there is a mechanic in the games code that sets the time frame in which the backflip must be done. After the player simply walks forward for a not so extended period of time (aproximately four tiles but most likely less) if they turn abruptly they will recieve a smallish speed boost that lasts just a small duration. If the player jumps right after the boost they will have successfully done a backflip! These can be useful in many ways, including traversing difficult rooms, accessing difficult rooms, and can turn a fight the outcome of a fight in your favor.


Another of the most simple mechanics in the game is the slide. The basic slide allows the player to get across large spaces quickly (*if done right ) and is also useful in the entering of tunnels or pipes. To slide, the player must crouch and jump nearly simultaneously and hold forward for the correct length (typically holding forward until the slide is over is acceptable, but fancier slides require different inputs). If the direction is not held long enough then you will have wasted your time.
